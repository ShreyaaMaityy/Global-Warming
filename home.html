WHAT IS GLOBAL WARMING ?

Global warming refers to the long-term increase in the Earth's average surface temperature caused by human activities, mainly the burning of fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes, which release large amounts of greenhouse gases (such as carbon dioxide, methane, and nitrous oxide) into the atmosphere. These gases trap heat from the sun, leading to an overall warming effect on the planet's temperature.
